===Etymology===
From {{etyl|fr|en}} {{m|fr|accommodation}} from {{etyl|la|en}} {{m|la|accommodātiō||adjustment, accommodation, compliance}}, from {{m|la|accommodō||adapt, put in order}}. Superficially {{suffix|accommodate|tion|lang=en}}. The sense of "lodging" was first attested in 1600.

===Noun===
{{en-noun|~}}

# {{senseid|en|lodging}} {{label|en|chiefly|British|usually|a mass noun}} [[lodging|Lodging]] in a [[dwelling]] or similar living quarters afforded to travellers in hotels or on cruise ships, or prisoners, etc.
# {{label|en|physical}} [[adaptation|Adaptation]] or [[adjustment]].
## {{label|en|countable|uncountable|followed by ''to''}} The act of fitting or adapting, or the state of being fitted or adapted; adaptation; adjustment.
##* {{rfdate}}, Sir {{w|Matthew Hale (jurist)|Matthew Hale}} (1609-1676)
##*: The organization of the body with '''accommodation''' to its functions.
## {{label|en|countable|uncountable}} A convenience, a fitting, something satisfying a need.
##* {{quote-book|year=1898|author={{w|Winston Churchill (novelist)|Winston Churchill}}
|title={{w|The Celebrity}}|chapter=10
|passage=Mr. Cooke had had a sloop yacht built at Far Harbor, the completion of which had been delayed, and which was but just delivered. […] The Maria had a cabin, which was finished in hard wood and yellow plush, and '''accommodations''' for keeping things cold.}}
## {{label|en|countable|physiology|biology}} The adaptation or adjustment of an organism, organ, or part.
## {{label|en|countable|medical}} The adjustment of the eye to a change of the distance from an observed object.
# {{label|en|personal}} [[adaptation|Adaptation]] or [[adjustment]].
## {{label|en|countable|uncountable}} Willingness to accommodate; obligingness.
## {{label|en|countable|uncountable}} Adjustment of differences; state of agreement; reconciliation; settlement; compromise.
##* {{rfdate}}, {{w|Thomas Babington Macaulay, 1st Baron Macaulay|Thomas Macaulay}} (1800-1859)
##*: To come to terms of '''accommodation'''.
## {{label|en|countable}} The application of a writer's language, on the ground of analogy, to something not originally referred to or intended.
##* {{rfdate}}, {{w|William Paley}} (1743-1805)
##: Many of those quotations from the Old Testament were probably intended as nothing more than '''accommodations'''.
## {{label|en|countable|commerce}} A loan of money.
## {{label|en|countable|commerce}} An accommodation bill or note.
## {{label|en|countable|legal}} An offer of [[substitute]] [[goods]] to [[fulfill]] a [[contract]], which will bind the [[purchaser]] if [[accepted]].
# {{label|en|countable|geology}} The place where sediments can make, or have made, a sedimentation.

====Derived terms====
: ''The definitions should be entered into dedicated entries for the terms defined.''
* [[accommodation bill]], or '''note,''' ''(Commerce):'' a bill of exchange which a person accepts, or a note which a person makes and delivers to another, not upon a consideration received, but for the purpose of raising money on credit
* [[accommodation coach]], or '''train:''' one running at moderate speed and stopping at all or nearly all stations
* [[accommodation ladder]], ''(Nautical):'' a light ladder hung over the side of a ship at the gangway, useful in ascending from, or descending to, small boats
* [[holiday accommodation]]
